About project

My role

  • UX-Designer
  • UX-Researcher

Project goal

The goal of the project is to create a mobile application that enables buyers to manage the delivery time and product composition based on their dietary preferences.

Target audience

Citizens with an active rhythm of life who like to eat on the go, but are not indifferent to quality and punctuality.

Key challenge

The key challenge was to avoid bias to take a fresh look at the well-known topic of fast food and, based on the real needs of the user, bring new benefits.

Reseach study details

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I anm designing for and their needs. The study identified two main groups describing user behavior and problems. One group of user users, identified as a result of the research, were adults and students who combine food with other activities and it is important for them that the ordering process does not require a lot of attention and does not break the planned schedule. Another type of user identified in the study reflects people who predominantly place orders in their free time, as part of their leisure time during city walks and tourist trips. They are sensitive to the problem of determining how tasty the food will be, what it consists of and whether it will suit their dietary preferences.

User testing results

I performed two rounds of usability studies, with the initial study findings helping to shape the design process from wireframes to mockups. The second study utilized a high-fidelity prototype and uncovered areas that required further refinement in the mockups.

Round I findings

  • 1. Users struggle to differentiate when a filter is active or inactive.
  • 2. The profile is complex and challenging for users to comprehend.
  • A majority of users may encounter issues accessing their order history.

Round II findings

  • 1. Users require a more distinct indication that the receipt process is finished and that all the steps were completed successfully.
  • 2. Users require additional confirmation of their actions during the checkout stage.

Conclusion

What I learned

While developing the SmartShaw application, I had the opportunity to gain systemic experience in designing the application. Each subsequent stage led to the need for some revision previous work. The full meaning of many stages becomes clear only now. Therefore, I find it necessary to outline a few further steps.

Next steps

  • 1. Review the sequence of project stages to ensure that all tasks have been completed and to identify any areas that may require further attention
  • 2. Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points user experienced have been affectively addressed.
  • 3. Perform refinement based on a improved and holistic vision. Make changes based on the results of a new usability study.
