About project

My role

  • UX-Designer
  • UX-Researcher

Project goal

The goal of the project is to create an adaptive web interface that allows users to visually and effortlessly configure air delivery options and monitor the progress of their delivery.

Target audience

The typical users of the product are individuals aged between 25 and 40 who reside in suburban or remote areas and prefer to save time and effort on shopping by having orders delivered to their homes.

Key challenge

It's a very innovative idea and it was important to stay in touch with reality and make the rather futuristic concept understandable and accessible. There was also a challenge related to the need to provide a responsive design while maintaining usability.

Reseach study details

I conducted user interviews, which I then turned into empathy maps to better understanding the target users and their needs. I have noticed that many target users find the regular delivery process as unpredictably and tedious. They expect innovative delivery methods to give them a sense of control and confidence in the first place, making them more independent in planning their day.

Initial design concepts

I sketched out paper wireframes for each screen in my app, mindful of user pain points related to the ease and visibility of order tracking. I was looking for a way to combine the two possibilities

  • 1. A simple and visual way to get the information you need
  • 2. Easy transition to the most detailed details, if required

Stars were used to designate the elements of each sketch that would be used in the initial digital wireframes. The selected concept based on the most successful elements.

Conclusion

What I learned

I've discovered that keeping the real user experience in mind is crucial for designing a solution that addresses their needs. It's important to simplify and make the design user-friendly to facilitate the user's transition to the new experience.

Next steps

  • 1. Consider secondary tasks identified during the usability research, if necessary, polish the application for a wider and more convenient functionality.
  • 2. Perform additional usability studies to confirm whether the issues that users found problematic have been effectively resolved
  • 3. Make changes based on the results of a new usability study. Identify any additional areas of need and ideate on new features.
